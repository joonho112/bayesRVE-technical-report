2  Notation, Acronyms, and Object Map

2.1 Mathematical Notation

This chapter collects every symbol used in the bayesRVE technical report. Symbols are grouped by topic and listed in order of first appearance. All vectors are column vectors unless transposed; all matrices use bold uppercase.

\(n_j\) Study size Number of effect sizes in study \(j\), \(j = 1, \ldots, J\) scalar Chapter 4
\(N\) Total observations \(N = \sum_{j=1}^{J} n_j\) scalar Chapter 4

\(\bar{\mathbf{x}}_j\) Study mean predictor \(\bar{\mathbf{x}}_j = n_j^{-1} \sum_{i=1}^{n_j} \mathbf{x}_{ij}\) \(K \times 1\) Section 4.2

\(S\) Number of MCMC draws Total posterior draws \(= \text{chains} \times \text{iter\_sampling}\) scalar Section 6.1

\(\boldsymbol{\psi}^{*(s)}\) CCC-calibrated draw Calibrated posterior draw (see Section 6.1) varies Section 6.1
\(\sigma_\beta\) Prior SD for \(\boldsymbol{\beta}\) Hyperparameter: \(\beta_k \sim \mathrm{N}(0, \sigma_\beta^2)\) scalar Chapter 4

2.1.3 3. Variance and Covariance Matrices

Symbol Name Definition Dimensions Introduced
\(v_j\) Marginal variance of \(\bar{y}_j\) \(v_j = \bar{s}_j^2 / n_j + \hat{\tau}^2\) (CE); \(v_j = (\bar{s}_j^2 + \hat{\omega}^2)/n_j + \hat{\tau}^2\) (CHE) scalar Section 4.2
\(a_j\) CHE within-study variance \(a_j = \bar{s}_j^2 + \hat{\omega}^2\) scalar Section 4.3
\(c_j\) CHE total variance factor \(c_j = a_j + n_j \hat{\tau}^2\) scalar Section 4.3
\(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_j\) Working covariance of study \(j\) \(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_j = \bar{s}_j^2 \mathbf{I}_{n_j} + \hat{\tau}^2 \mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}'\) (CE); \(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}_j = a_j \mathbf{I}_{n_j} + \hat{\tau}^2 \mathbf{1}\mathbf{1}'\) (CHE) \(n_j \times n_j\) Chapter 6
\(\mathbf{W}_j\) Weight matrix \(\mathbf{W}_j = \boldsymbol{\Sigma}_j^{-1}\), computed via Woodbury identity \(n_j \times n_j\) Section 6.5
\(\mathbf{W}_j^{1/2}\) Weight matrix square root Symmetric positive-definite matrix s.t. \(\mathbf{W}_j^{1/2}\mathbf{W}_j^{1/2} = \mathbf{W}_j\); analytical via rank-1 formula (D24) \(n_j \times n_j\) Section 6.5
\(\mathbf{W}_j^{-1/2}\) Inverse weight square root \((\mathbf{W}_j^{1/2})^{-1}\) \(n_j \times n_j\) Section 6.5
\(\hat{\boldsymbol{\Sigma}}_{\mathrm{MCMC}}\) MCMC posterior covariance Sample covariance of posterior draws: \(\operatorname{Cov}_S[\boldsymbol{\psi}^{(1)}, \ldots, \boldsymbol{\psi}^{(S)}]\); CCC uses only the beta-block \(\hat{\boldsymbol{\Sigma}}_{\mathrm{MCMC}}^{(\beta)}\) (\(K \times K\)) \((K\!+\!J) \times (K\!+\!J)\) Section 6.1
\(V_{\mathrm{HC0}}\) Uncorrected sandwich (beta block) HC0 sandwich estimator for \(\boldsymbol{\beta}\): \(\mathbf{H}^{-1}\mathbf{M}\mathbf{H}^{-1}\) restricted to rows/cols \(1{:}K\) \(K \times K\) Chapter 6
\(V_{\mathrm{CR2}}\) CR2 bias-reduced sandwich \(V_{\mathrm{CR2}} = \mathbf{Q}\bigl(\sum_{j=1}^{J} \tilde{\mathbf{s}}_j \tilde{\mathbf{s}}_j'\bigr)\mathbf{Q}\) (Bell & McCaffrey, 2002; Tipton, 2015) \(K \times K\) Section 6.5
\(V_{\mathrm{sand}}\) Sandwich variance (generic) Context-dependent: \(V_{\mathrm{CR2}}\) when small_sample = "CR2", else \(V_{\mathrm{HC0}}\) \(K \times K\) Chapter 6
\(\mathbf{V}_{\mathrm{sand,full}}\) Full marginal sandwich Sandwich for \(\boldsymbol{\phi} = (\boldsymbol{\beta}', \tau^2)'\) (CE) or \((\boldsymbol{\beta}', \tau^2, \omega^2)'\) (CHE) \((K\!+\!1) \times (K\!+\!1)\) or \((K\!+\!2) \times (K\!+\!2)\) Chapter 6
\(V_{\mathrm{sand,cond},j}\) Conditional sandwich for \(\theta_j\) Per-study conditional sandwich: \(V_j = u_j^2 / H_j^2\) (CE only) scalar Chapter 6

2.1.4 4. Sandwich Internals (Bread and Meat)

Symbol Name Definition Dimensions Introduced
\(\mathbf{s}_j^{(\beta)}\) Beta score for study \(j\) Likelihood score: \(\mathbf{s}_j^{(\beta)} = \sum_i (r_{ij}/w_{ij})\,\mathbf{x}_{ij}\) (obs-level) or \((\bar{r}_j / v_j)\,\bar{\mathbf{x}}_j\) (study-level) \(K \times 1\) Chapter 6
\(s_j^{(\tau^2)}\) Tau-squared score for study \(j\) \(s_j^{(\tau^2)} = -1/(2v_j) + \bar{r}_j^2/(2v_j^2)\) scalar Chapter 6
\(s_j^{(\omega^2)}\) Omega-squared score (CHE) Score for \(\omega^2\) in the CHE marginal likelihood scalar Section 4.3
\(\mathbf{S}\) Score matrix \(\mathbf{S} = [\mathbf{s}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{s}_J]'\) where \(\mathbf{s}_j = (\mathbf{s}_j^{(\beta)\prime}, s_j^{(\tau^2)}, \ldots)'\) \(J \times d\) Chapter 6
\(\mathbf{M}\) Meat matrix \(\mathbf{M} = \mathbf{S}'\mathbf{S} = \sum_{j=1}^{J} \mathbf{s}_j \mathbf{s}_j'\) (likelihood scores only; prior excluded) \(d \times d\) Chapter 6
\(\mathbf{H}_{\mathrm{lik}}\) Likelihood Hessian \(-\partial^2 \log p(\mathbf{y} \mid \boldsymbol{\phi}) / \partial \boldsymbol{\phi}\,\partial \boldsymbol{\phi}'\) (positive-definite convention) \(d \times d\) Chapter 6
\(\mathbf{H}_{\mathrm{prior}}\) Stabilized prior Hessian \(\mathrm{diag}(1/\sigma_\beta^2, \ldots, 1/\sigma_\beta^2, \,h_{\tau^2})\); the \(\tau^2\) entry \(h_{\tau^2}\) is a numerical ridge stabilizer (capped at \(10^6\) when \(\hat{\tau}^2 \approx 0\)), not the exact prior second derivative \(d \times d\) Chapter 6
\(\mathbf{H}_{\mathrm{post}}\) Posterior Hessian (bread) \(\mathbf{H}_{\mathrm{post}} = \mathbf{H}_{\mathrm{lik}} + \mathbf{H}_{\mathrm{prior}}\) \(d \times d\) Chapter 6
\(\mathbf{Q}\) WLS bread \(\mathbf{Q} = (\mathbf{X}'\mathbf{W}\mathbf{X})^{-1} = \bigl(\sum_j \mathbf{X}_j'\mathbf{W}_j\mathbf{X}_j\bigr)^{-1}\) (fixed-effect WLS, D15) \(K \times K\) Section 6.5
\(H_j\) Conditional Hessian for \(\theta_j\) \(H_j = n_j / \bar{s}_j^2 + 1/\hat{\tau}^2\) (CE) scalar Chapter 6
\(u_j\) Conditional score for \(\theta_j\) \(u_j = (n_j / \bar{s}_j^2)\,r_{\mathrm{cond},j}\) where \(r_{\mathrm{cond},j} = \bar{y}_j - \bar{\mathbf{x}}_j'\hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}} - \hat{\theta}_j\) scalar Chapter 6
\(d\) Marginal parameter dimension \(d = K + 1\) (CE) or \(d = K + 2\) (CHE) scalar Chapter 6

2.1.5 5. CCC Objects (Cholesky Calibration Correction)

Symbol Name Definition Dimensions Introduced
\(L_1\) MCMC Cholesky factor \(L_1 = \mathrm{chol}(\hat{\boldsymbol{\Sigma}}_{\mathrm{MCMC},\beta})\), lower-triangular \(K \times K\) Section 6.1
\(L_2\) Sandwich Cholesky factor \(L_2 = \mathrm{chol}(V_{\mathrm{sand}})\), lower-triangular \(K \times K\) Section 6.1
\(\mathbf{A}_\beta\) Beta-block CCC transformation \(\mathbf{A}_\beta = L_2 L_1^{-1}\) \(K \times K\) Section 6.1
\(\boldsymbol{\beta}^{*(s)}\) CCC-calibrated beta draw \(\boldsymbol{\beta}^{*(s)} = \hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}} + \mathbf{A}_\beta(\boldsymbol{\beta}^{(s)} - \hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}})\) \(K \times 1\) Section 6.1
\(r_\theta^{(j)}\) Theta rescaling factor \(r_\theta^{(j)} = \sqrt{V_{\mathrm{sand,cond},j} / \mathrm{Var}_{\mathrm{MCMC}}(\theta_j)}\) (CE only) scalar Section 6.1
\(R_k\) Per-parameter SD ratio \(R_k = \mathrm{SD}(\psi_k^{*}) / \mathrm{SD}(\psi_k)\) for parameter \(k\) scalar Section 6.1
\(\|\mathbf{A}_\beta - \mathbf{I}_K\|_F\) Frobenius perturbation Measures departure of CCC from identity (no calibration needed \(\Rightarrow 0\)) scalar Section 6.1
\(\hat{\boldsymbol{\Sigma}}_{\mathrm{MCMC},\beta}\) Beta-block MCMC covariance Sample covariance of \(\boldsymbol{\beta}^{(1)}, \ldots, \boldsymbol{\beta}^{(S)}\) \(K \times K\) Section 6.1
\(\operatorname{Cov}_S(\cdot)\) Sample covariance operator \(\operatorname{Cov}_S(\boldsymbol{\beta}^*) = (S\!-\!1)^{-1}\sum_{s}(\boldsymbol{\beta}^{*(s)} - \bar{\boldsymbol{\beta}}^*)(\boldsymbol{\beta}^{*(s)} - \bar{\boldsymbol{\beta}}^*)'\) context-dep. Section 6.1

2.1.6 6. Small-Sample Inference (CR2 and Satterthwaite)

Symbol Name Definition Dimensions Introduced
\(\mathbf{A}_j\) CR2 adjustment matrix Bias-correction matrix s.t. \(\mathrm{E}[\mathbf{A}_j \hat{\mathbf{e}}_j \hat{\mathbf{e}}_j' \mathbf{A}_j'] = \boldsymbol{\Sigma}_j\) \(n_j \times n_j\) Section 6.5
\(\tilde{\mathbf{H}}_{jj}\) Symmetrized hat block \(\tilde{\mathbf{H}}_{jj} = \mathbf{W}_j^{1/2}\mathbf{X}_j\mathbf{Q}\mathbf{X}_j'\mathbf{W}_j^{1/2}\) \(n_j \times n_j\) Section 6.5
\(\tilde{\mathbf{A}}_j\) Symmetrized CR2 adjustment \(\tilde{\mathbf{A}}_j = (\mathbf{I}_{n_j} - \tilde{\mathbf{H}}_{jj})^{-1/2}\) (eigendecomposition) \(n_j \times n_j\) Section 6.5
\(\mathbf{H}_{jj}\) Hat matrix block \(\mathbf{H}_{jj} = \mathbf{X}_j \mathbf{Q} \mathbf{X}_j' \mathbf{W}_j\) (not symmetric in general) \(n_j \times n_j\) Section 6.5
\(h_j\) Study leverage \(h_j = \bar{\mathbf{x}}_j' \mathbf{Q} \bar{\mathbf{x}}_j / v_j\) (study-level) or \(\mathrm{tr}(\mathbf{H}_{jj})/n_j\) (obs-level) scalar Section 6.5
\(\tilde{\mathbf{e}}_j\) CR2-adjusted residual \(\tilde{\mathbf{e}}_j = \mathbf{A}_j \hat{\mathbf{e}}_j\) \(n_j \times 1\) Section 6.5
\(\tilde{\mathbf{s}}_j\) CR2-adjusted score \(\tilde{\mathbf{s}}_j = \mathbf{X}_j'\mathbf{W}_j \tilde{\mathbf{e}}_j = \mathbf{X}_j'\mathbf{W}_j\mathbf{A}_j\hat{\mathbf{e}}_j\) \(K \times 1\) Section 6.5
\(\mathbf{M}_{\mathrm{CR2}}\) CR2 meat matrix \(\mathbf{M}_{\mathrm{CR2}} = \sum_{j=1}^{J} \tilde{\mathbf{s}}_j \tilde{\mathbf{s}}_j'\) \(K \times K\) Section 6.5
\(\boldsymbol{\Lambda}_\beta\) Beta prior precision \(\boldsymbol{\Lambda}_\beta = \mathrm{diag}(1/\sigma_\beta^2, \ldots, 1/\sigma_\beta^2)\) \(K \times K\) Chapter 6
\(\mathbf{B}_\beta\) Penalized precision \(\mathbf{B}_\beta = \bar{\mathbf{X}}'\mathbf{W}\bar{\mathbf{X}} + \boldsymbol{\Lambda}_\beta\) (Bayesian leverage computation) \(K \times K\) Chapter 6
\(c_\beta\) Scalar small-sample correction \(c_\beta = J / (J - p_{\mathrm{eff}})\) scalar Chapter 6
\(p_{\mathrm{eff}}\) Effective parameters \(p_{\mathrm{eff}} = \sum_{j=1}^{J} h_j\) (sum of Bayesian leverages) scalar Chapter 6
\(w_j\) Study weight \(w_j = 1/v_j\) (inverse marginal variance) scalar Chapter 6
\(J_{\min}\) Operating envelope \(J_{\min} = \max(8,\, 2K + 2)\) (D7: minimum studies for sandwich) scalar Chapter 6
\(\mathrm{df}_k\) Satterthwaite df \(\mathrm{df}_k = [\mathrm{tr}(\mathbf{P}_k)]^2 / \mathrm{tr}(\mathbf{P}_k^2)\) for coefficient \(k\) scalar Section 6.6
\(\mathbf{P}_k\) Satterthwaite \(P\) matrix \(J \times J\) matrix with entries \(P_{ij}\) depending on \(\mathbf{g}_{jk}\) vectors (deterministic conditional on plug-in \(\hat{\tau}^2, \hat{\omega}^2\)) \(J \times J\) Section 6.6
\(\mathbf{g}_{jk}\) Satterthwaite \(g\) vector \(\mathbf{g}_{jk} = \mathbf{A}_j'\mathbf{W}_j\mathbf{X}_j\mathbf{Q}\mathbf{c}_k\) where \(\mathbf{c}_k\) is column \(k\) of \(\mathbf{I}_K\) \(n_j \times 1\) Section 6.6

### 4. Sandwich Internals (Bread and Meat)

| Symbol | Name | Definition | Dimensions | Introduced |
|:-------|:-----|:-----------|:-----------|:-----------|
| $\mathbf{s}_j^{(\beta)}$ | Beta score for study $j$ | Likelihood score: $\mathbf{s}_j^{(\beta)} = \sum_i (r_{ij}/w_{ij})\,\mathbf{x}_{ij}$ (obs-level) or $(\bar{r}_j / v_j)\,\bar{\mathbf{x}}_j$ (study-level) | $K \times 1$ | @sec-sandwich |
| $s_j^{(\tau^2)}$ | Tau-squared score for study $j$ | $s_j^{(\tau^2)} = -1/(2v_j) + \bar{r}_j^2/(2v_j^2)$ | scalar | @sec-sandwich |
| $s_j^{(\omega^2)}$ | Omega-squared score (CHE) | Score for $\omega^2$ in the CHE marginal likelihood | scalar | @sec-che-model |
| $\mathbf{S}$ | Score matrix | $\mathbf{S} = [\mathbf{s}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{s}_J]'$ where $\mathbf{s}_j = (\mathbf{s}_j^{(\beta)\prime}, s_j^{(\tau^2)}, \ldots)'$ | $J \times d$ | @sec-sandwich |
| $\mathbf{M}$ | Meat matrix | $\mathbf{M} = \mathbf{S}'\mathbf{S} = \sum_{j=1}^{J} \mathbf{s}_j \mathbf{s}_j'$ (likelihood scores only; prior excluded) | $d \times d$ | @sec-sandwich |
| $\mathbf{H}_{\mathrm{lik}}$ | Likelihood Hessian | $-\partial^2 \log p(\mathbf{y} \mid \boldsymbol{\phi}) / \partial \boldsymbol{\phi}\,\partial \boldsymbol{\phi}'$ (positive-definite convention) | $d \times d$ | @sec-sandwich |
| $\mathbf{H}_{\mathrm{prior}}$ | Stabilized prior Hessian | $\mathrm{diag}(1/\sigma_\beta^2, \ldots, 1/\sigma_\beta^2, \,h_{\tau^2})$; the $\tau^2$ entry $h_{\tau^2}$ is a numerical ridge stabilizer (capped at $10^6$ when $\hat{\tau}^2 \approx 0$), not the exact prior second derivative | $d \times d$ | @sec-sandwich |
| $\mathbf{H}_{\mathrm{post}}$ | Posterior Hessian (bread) | $\mathbf{H}_{\mathrm{post}} = \mathbf{H}_{\mathrm{lik}} + \mathbf{H}_{\mathrm{prior}}$ | $d \times d$ | @sec-sandwich |
| $\mathbf{Q}$ | WLS bread | $\mathbf{Q} = (\mathbf{X}'\mathbf{W}\mathbf{X})^{-1} = \bigl(\sum_j \mathbf{X}_j'\mathbf{W}_j\mathbf{X}_j\bigr)^{-1}$ (fixed-effect WLS, D15) | $K \times K$ | @sec-cr2 |
| $H_j$ | Conditional Hessian for $\theta_j$ | $H_j = n_j / \bar{s}_j^2 + 1/\hat{\tau}^2$ (CE) | scalar | @sec-sandwich |
| $u_j$ | Conditional score for $\theta_j$ | $u_j = (n_j / \bar{s}_j^2)\,r_{\mathrm{cond},j}$ where $r_{\mathrm{cond},j} = \bar{y}_j - \bar{\mathbf{x}}_j'\hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}} - \hat{\theta}_j$ | scalar | @sec-sandwich |
| $d$ | Marginal parameter dimension | $d = K + 1$ (CE) or $d = K + 2$ (CHE) | scalar | @sec-sandwich |
