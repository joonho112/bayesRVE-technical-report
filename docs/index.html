Technical Report — Theory, Implementation, and Empirical Validation

Author

JoonHo Lee

Published

March 26, 2026

The Gap Between Bayesian and Robust Inference

Applied researchers increasingly turn to Bayesian methods for meta-analysis with dependent effect sizes. Bayesian hierarchical models offer natural handling of complex dependence structures, coherent uncertainty propagation through variance components, and direct probability statements about quantities of interest. Yet these advantages rest on the assumption that the working model is correctly specified—an assumption that is rarely defensible when effect sizes are statistically dependent within studies.

Frequentist meta-analysts have long addressed this vulnerability through robust variance estimation (RVE). The CR2 sandwich estimator, combined with Satterthwaite degrees of freedom, provides confidence intervals that maintain nominal coverage regardless of the true within-study correlation structure. However, the resulting inference sacrifices the Bayesian advantages of shrinkage estimation and probabilistic interpretation.

The fundamental tension, then, is between two desirable properties: the shrinkage and probabilistic coherence of Bayesian point estimation, and the misspecification-robust coverage guarantees of sandwich-based inference. This report develops and validates Cholesky Calibration Correction (CCC) as a principled resolution of this tension.

Purpose and Scope

This report provides a self-contained account of the theory, implementation, and empirical validation of the bayesRVE R package (version 0.6.0), which implements CCC for Bayesian meta-analysis with dependent effect sizes. The intended audience includes methodologists working at the intersection of Bayesian computation and robust variance estimation, as well as applied researchers seeking calibrated inference from multi-level meta-analytic models.

The CCC transformation takes the form \[ \boldsymbol{\psi}^{*(s)} = \hat{\boldsymbol{\psi}} + \mathbf{L}_2 \mathbf{L}_1^{-1}(\boldsymbol{\psi}^{(s)} - \hat{\boldsymbol{\psi}}), \tag{1}\] where \(\mathbf{L}_1 = \text{chol}(\hat{\boldsymbol{\Sigma}}_{\text{MCMC}})\) is the Cholesky factor of the posterior covariance, \(\mathbf{L}_2 = \text{chol}(\mathbf{V}_{\text{CR2}})\) is the Cholesky factor of the CR2 sandwich variance, and \(\hat{\boldsymbol{\psi}}\) is the posterior mean. This affine map preserves the posterior mean as the point estimate while replacing the model-dependent posterior spread with a misspecification-robust alternative.

Three-Layer Calibration

The bayesRVE package implements a three-layer calibration framework:

  • Layer 1 (Location). The posterior mean \(\hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}} = \mathbb{E}[\boldsymbol{\beta} \mid \mathbf{y}]\) serves as the point estimate. For both correlated-effects (CE) and correlated-hierarchical-effects (CHE) models, the posterior mean incorporates partial pooling across studies, yielding shrinkage estimates that borrow strength from the ensemble.

  • Layer 2 (Spread). CCC transforms the posterior draws so that \(\text{Cov}(\boldsymbol{\beta}^*) = \mathbf{V}_{\text{CR2}}\) exactly. The posterior spread is recalibrated from model-dependent to sandwich-robust, while the posterior mean is preserved as an algebraic identity (sec-ccc, Theorem 1).

  • Layer 3 (Tail behavior). Satterthwaite degrees of freedom \(\text{df}_k\), computed from the CR2 adjustment matrices, determine the reference distribution for each coefficient. The resulting CCC-\(t\) intervals, \[ \hat{\beta}_k \pm t_{\alpha/2,\, \text{df}_k} \cdot \text{SD}(\beta_k^*), \] are Bayesian in their point estimate, sandwich-robust in their width, and small-sample corrected in their tail behavior.

What This Report Demonstrates

The principal claims, developed across the thirteen chapters that follow, are:

  1. Exact covariance alignment. CCC is an affine transformation whose empirical covariance matrix exactly equals the CR2 sandwich variance matrix (Theorem 2, sec-ccc). This result is verified numerically at machine precision on synthetic data.

  2. Generality across working models. Three-Layer Calibration applies to both the CE model (\(\tau^2\) only) and the CHE model (\(\tau^2 + \omega^2\)). The CHE extension requires only the substitution \(\bar{s}_j^2 \to \bar{s}_j^2 + \hat{\omega}^2\) into the weight matrices; no new mathematical machinery is needed (sec-models).

  3. Plug-in parity with established software. When REML variance components are injected into the bayesRVE CR2 machinery, the resulting sandwich variance matches the clubSandwich package to a tolerance of \(10^{-6}\), isolating the linear algebra from MCMC sampling noise (sec-verification).

  4. Real-data validation. On the Tanner-Smith & Lipsey (2015) dataset (\(J = 107\) studies, \(N = 1{,}198\) effect sizes), the ratio of CCC posterior standard deviation to CR2 standard error is approximately 1.0 across all moderator categories, for both CE and CHE models (sec-results).

  5. Comprehensive automated testing. The bayesRVE test suite comprises 2,701+ expectations across 39 test files, covering algebraic identities on synthetic data, MCMC-based integration tests, edge cases, and backward compatibility guarantees (sec-verification).
